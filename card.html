Cat Meowing

Domestic cats use many vocalizations for communication, including purring, trilling, grunting, and several different forms of meowing.

  • Sleep longer: 20 hours
  • Max purring intensity: 90db
  • US pop range: from 25 to 60 million
  • Global pop range: from 200 to 600 million

The tail and ears are particularly important social signal mechanisms in cats, for example a raised tail acts as a friendly greeting and flattened ears indicates hostility.

Nose-to-nose touching is also a common greeting and may be followed by social grooming, which is solicited by one of the cats raising and tilting its head.
